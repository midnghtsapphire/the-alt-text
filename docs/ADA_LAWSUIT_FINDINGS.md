# ADA Website Accessibility Lawsuit Research

**Research Date:** January 29, 2026

---

## Key Statistics

- **2024 Lawsuits:** Over 4,000 lawsuits filed in federal and state courts
- **Legal Basis:** ADA Title III (42 U.S.C. 12182(a)) - "Prohibition of discrimination by public accommodations"
- **Primary Jurisdictions:** New York, Florida, California (majority of cases)
- **Recent Growth:** Significant increase in 7th Circuit (Wisconsin, Illinois, Indiana)

---

## Common Digital Barriers Cited in Lawsuits

1. **Screen Reader Incompatibility** - Websites not compatible with screen-reader software
2. **Missing Alt Text** - Absence of "alternate text" for images
3. **Video/Audio Issues** - Missing captions or transcripts
4. **Color Contrast** - Insufficient color contrast
5. **Navigation Errors** - Broken links, pop-ups, widgets causing keyboard navigation errors
6. **Inaccessible Forms** - Forms that cannot be completed with assistive technology
7. **Wait Times** - Supplementary call lines with unreasonable wait times

---

## Legal Landscape - Three Circuit Court Approaches

### Approach 1: Physical Nexus Required (3rd, 5th, 6th, 9th Circuits)
- Company can only be sued if digital site has sufficient "nexus" to a physical location
- Strong connection required between website and actual physical location
- Retail stores or public accommodations with websites fall under this

### Approach 2: No Physical Location Required (1st, 2nd, 7th Circuits)
- Websites can be sued under ADA regardless of physical presence
- Online-only businesses are covered
- Most plaintiff-friendly interpretation

### Approach 3: Not Covered (11th Circuit)
- Websites not considered "places of public accommodation"
- Most restrictive interpretation
- Least common approach

## Compliance Standards

### WCAG 2.1 (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)
- **Level A:** Minimum accessibility (basic requirements)
- **Level AA:** Mid-range accessibility (widely considered legally acceptable)
- **Level AAA:** Highest accessibility (most comprehensive)

**Industry Standard:** WCAG 2.1 Level AA is the minimum standard for compliance

---

## Business Risk Factors

### High-Risk Industries
1. Retail/E-commerce
2. Restaurants
3. Healthcare providers
4. Financial services
5. Hotels/Hospitality
6. Entertainment venues
7. Educational institutions
8. Government contractors

### Risk Indicators
- No accessibility statement
- No keyboard navigation support
- Images without alt text
- Videos without captions
- Forms without proper labels
- Poor color contrast
- No skip navigation links

---

## Settlement Patterns

- Most cases settle out of court
- Settlements typically include:
  - Website remediation costs
  - Attorney fees (often $10,000-$50,000+)
  - Plaintiff damages
  - Ongoing monitoring agreements
  - Commitment to maintain accessibility

---

## Compliance Requirements

### Federal
- **ADA Title III** - Public accommodations must be accessible
- **Section 508** - Federal government websites (mandatory WCAG 2.0 AA)

### State Laws
- Some states have additional accessibility requirements
- California, New York, Florida have most active enforcement

---

## Business Impact

### Costs of Non-Compliance
- Legal fees: $10,000-$100,000+
- Settlement amounts: Variable
- Remediation costs: $5,000-$50,000+ depending on site size
- Reputation damage
- Lost customers (15% of population has disabilities)

### Benefits of Compliance
- Avoid lawsuits
- Expand customer base (+15% potential customers)
- Improve SEO (accessibility features help search engines)
- Better user experience for all users
- Competitive advantage
- Corporate social responsibility
